# User Knowledge Prerequisites (Knowledge Check)

Before using the **HanapMedisina** application, users are expected to have a baseline understanding of basic smartphone operations and safety awareness. Here is the "Knowledge Check" outlining what users should know prior to using the app:

## 1. Basic Technical Literacy
*   **Smartphone Navigation:** Users must know how to navigate standard mobile interfaces (e.g., tapping tabs, scrolling through lists, and using search bars).
*   **Account Management:** Users should understand how to create an account using an email address and password, and how to log in securely.
*   **Granting Permissions:** Users must know how to grant the app necessary system permissions, specifically the **Camera** permission (required for scanning plants) and **Storage/Gallery** permission (if updating profile pictures).

## 2. App-Specific Awareness
*   **Online vs. Offline States:** Users should understand the concept of internet connectivity. They need to know that while they can scan plants *offline* in the field, they must reconnect to the internet later to back up their scans and browse the full database.
*   **Syncing Concept:** Users should be familiar with the idea of "syncing" or backing up data to the cloud when prompted by the app.

## 3. Best Practices for Scanning
*   **Camera Operation:** Users should know how to hold the phone steady and use tap-to-focus to capture clear, well-lit images.
*   **Subject Focus:** Users should understand that the AI model works best when the camera is focused on the distinguishing features of a plant (such as a clear view of a leaf's shape, the stem, or flowers) rather than a distant or blurry bush.

## 4. Safety and Medical Awareness (Crucial)
*   **Informational Purpose:** Users must understand that the AI is a tool for *guidance and education*, not a certified botanist or doctor. Machine learning models can make mistakes.
*   **Consumption Warning:** Users must know **never** to ingest, apply, or medically use a plant based *solely* on the app's identification without consulting a healthcare professional or an expert herbalist first. 
*   **Look-alikes:** Users should be aware that many medicinal plants have toxic look-alikes. They should know to utilize the app's "Compare" feature and "Physical Checklist" to verify plant identity manually.
